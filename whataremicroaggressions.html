What are microaggressions?

MMicroaggressions are little verbal comments but can also be nonverbal, which insults people. Mostly they affect people of color, people with a migration background, people who are part of the LGBTQ community, people who are in a lower social class, members of a certain religion or people with a physical disability. They can really affect everyone.

They express racism or sexism but sometimes they are not intended to be racist. Nearly every time the assaulter isn’t aware what he or she really is saying. Mostly, the attacker thinks what he or she is saying is a compliment although it is insulting. To avoid this everyone should know what microaggressions are. If you say the that the things he or she said can hurt people they try to play it down or say it was just a joke.

The psychologist Derald Wing Sue from Columbia University is one of the key experts on microaggressions. He did a lot of scientific work on this topic and divided Microaggressions in three types, which we will explain later. Microaggressions are a socio-psychological term describing little insulting comments in normal live.

This are the three types of microaggressions:

Microassaults:

Microassaults are verbal comments with the intention to hurt someone. They also can be nonverbal and are quite like classic direct racism. That’s why they aren’t important in the scientific research. An example:

“Men walking around outside at night are rapist.”

Microinsults:

Microinsults are comments that mostly aren’t intended to be discriminating. The assaulter usually isn’t aware about their prejudices and thinks he isn’t racist or sexist or anything like that. The comments are typically supposed to be a compliment even though they aren’t. A microinsult includes an assumption to a stereotype. An example:

“As a girl you are really good at programming.”

Microinvalidations:

Microinvalidation ignore or belittle thoughts and feelings of some people. They aren’t intentional and ignore the individual with their feelings and experiences. An example:

“For me, all people are the same. I don’t see colors.”

Remember:

Microaggressions are little verbal comments which insults people mostly without intending to do. They also can be nonverbal. There are three types of microaggressions.

Microassaults are intentional discriminating the other.

Microinsults are not intended and supposed to be a compliment but include an exception to a stereotype.

Microinvalidations aren’t intentional and ignore the individual with their thoughts, feelings and experiences.
