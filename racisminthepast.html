Racism in the past

At the beginning of the colonial times many countries especially in Europe and the USA went to Africa and enslaved people. They wanted them to work for them under bad conditions without giving them any choice. But these people had to justify why they did so. Because of that they misinterpreted the theories of Charles Darwin. They said that African and black people are a different race than white people and worth less.

This way of thinking continued during the National Socialism in Germany and got very extreme. The German government said that people who aren’t Arian i.e. not having German blood such as e.g. Caucasian or Jewish people, would be worth less and should therefore not be part of the community. The government increased this kind of racism especially against Jewish people. But as many times there wasn’t a profound reason for this racism. So, we have to stop racist beliefs and body of thought.

Remember:

Slavery had a huge impact in the history of racism. The enslavement of African people was justified because of a misinterpretation of the theories of Charles Darwin. The National Socialism in Germany also strengthened the racist thinking of some people.
